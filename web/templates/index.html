Understanding Your Portfolio

Investment terms explained in plain English

Total Return

This is how much money you've made (or lost) overall. If you invested $1,000 and now have $1,100, your total return is 10%. Simple as that!

Volatility

Think of this as the "bumpiness" of your investment ride. High volatility means your portfolio value swings up and down a lot. Low volatility means it's more stable and predictable.

Mean Return

The average return of a stock over time. If a stock goes up 5% one month and down 3% the next, the mean return helps you see the "typical" performance.

Weight

What percentage of your money is in each stock. If you have $1,000 total and $300 in Apple stock, Apple has a 30% weight in your portfolio.

Value at Risk (VaR)

The maximum amount you could lose on a bad day (with 95% confidence). If your VaR is 5%, you could lose up to 5% of your investment on a really bad day.

Diversification

The classic "don't put all your eggs in one basket" rule. Spreading your money across different stocks reduces risk because if one crashes, you don't lose everything.

Pro Tip: Lower volatility and higher returns are ideal, but they rarely come together. Most investments are a balance between risk and reward!

How It Works

1. Upload Your Portfolio

Export your holdings as a CSV file from your brokerage (Robinhood, TD Ameritrade, etc.) and upload it to Portfolia.

2. Data Processing

Our system fetches real-time historical stock data using Yahoo Finance API and processes it through our C++ risk engine for lightning-fast calculations.

3. Risk Analysis

The engine calculates key metrics including volatility, Value at Risk (VaR), mean returns, and portfolio correlations using advanced financial mathematics.

4. AI Interpretation

Google's Gemini AI analyzes your results and provides personalized insights, comparing your performance against the S&P 500 and offering actionable recommendations.

Technical Architecture

Backend: Flask (Python)
Risk Engine: C++ (High-performance calculations)
Frontend: JavaScript, Chart.js
Data Source: Yahoo Finance API
AI: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash
Database: SQLite
